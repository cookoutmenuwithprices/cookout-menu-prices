About Cook Out Restaurant

Cook Out is an American fast-food chain that was founded in 1989 in Greensboro, North Carolina by Morris Reaves. What started as a single drive-thru window has grown into one of the most beloved regional fast-food brands in America, with over 350 locations spread across the Southeastern United States.

The chain is now led by Morris's son, Jeremy Reaves, who has stayed true to the original vision: serve fresh, char-grilled food at prices that nobody else can match. Cook Out restaurants operate exclusively in the Southeast — North Carolina, South Carolina, Virginia, Georgia, Tennessee, Kentucky, Alabama, Mississippi, West Virginia, Maryland, Florida, and several other states. If you live in or visit these areas, Cook Out is genuinely one of the best fast-food experiences available.

There are three things that make the Cookout restaurant menu stand apart from every competitor in the fast-food industry:

  1. Value — The Cookout Tray ($7.39) delivers more food per dollar than any combo meal at McDonald's, Wendy's, Chick-fil-A, or Burger King.
  2. Milkshakes — 40+ thick, hand-spun flavors all at a flat $3.99 through the Cook Out Creamery. No other fast-food chain comes close in variety or quality.
  3. Southern authenticity — Homemade chili, fresh coleslaw, char-grilled beef, slow-cooked BBQ pulled pork, and hush puppies. This is real Southern food at drive-thru speed.

Cook Out does not serve breakfast. Most locations open at 10:30 AM and stay open until 1:00 AM–4:00 AM depending on the day and location, making it a favourite late-night destination for college students, families, and night-shift workers throughout the South.

Why Is Cookout So Cheap?

Cook Out keeps prices low through a combination of smart business decisions that have never changed since 1989. The chain operates a low-overhead, drive-thru-only model with minimal décor and seating. Their operations are extremely efficient with a focused menu that limits food waste. Founder Morris Reaves built the entire brand philosophy around providing maximum value — and his son Jeremy continues that tradition. Cook Out has never compromised on quality to cut costs; they have instead cut costs everywhere else to protect quality.
